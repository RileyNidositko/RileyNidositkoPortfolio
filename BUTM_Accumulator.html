Terrier Motorsport Accumulator Cart

This project was for the Boston University Terrier Motorsport team, which is BU's Formula Hybrid+Electric SAE team. The goal of the project was to build a cart which can safely transport and lift the car battery into the chassis from below while remaining rules compliant.

Skills:

Solidworks CAD, Mechanical Design, Project Planning, Welding, Machining

Important Requirements:

Must cost less than $400, must be electrically insulated, cart must have a dead man's brake, minimum table height of 12 in. when fully compressed, load Capacity of at least 56 kg with a FOS of 1.5, no wider than 26 in, and brake operation force under 10 lb.

Outcome:

After lots of different designs and trial and error, all metrics were met and the project was finished in time to be used at competition. The chosen solution was to purchase an existing hydraulic cart that was capable of lifting the battery and modify it to have a dead man's brake to minimize cost and design time. Through this project I practced CAD design with Solidworks and machining with a manual lathe. I learned how to design around existing components while also improving my MIG welding and project planning skills.
